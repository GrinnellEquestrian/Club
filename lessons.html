Lessons

What makes someone a "member of equestrian club" is taking riding lessons, since this is how all of us improve our riding skills and learn more about horses. Drill team members take riding lessons because although practices are great for learning drills and moves, lessons are far better for learning more about our horses and improving our riding. Most members of Equestrian Club take lessons at Triple V because of its convenient location, but lessons are also available in nearby towns for students who are looking for more serious English instruction.

If you used to take lessons with Heidi or Grace, please note that they are not teaching lessons anymore, but any of our other instructors would love to teach you!

Cost

Although we wish it could be free, using a schoolhorse, the arena, and an hour of the instructor's time costs money, and that's out of our control. It adds up to $20 per lesson, but thanks to support from Grinnell College, students are reimbursed $12. Yes, it really is $8 for an hour of quality teaching! That's why Triple V is one of the least expensive places to learn to ride. If this cost is keeping you from taking lessons, let us know, and we will work something out with you. It's important to us that everyone who wants to ride gets a chance!

Policies

There are a few strict policies that all students must adhere to, including drill team riders. Please familiarize yourself with these policies before you commit to taking lessons. We don't have these rules to be mean. It's to allow equestrian club to run as smoothly as possible.
